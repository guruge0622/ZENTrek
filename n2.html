SINHARAJA RAIN FOREST

The Sinharaja Rainforest, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is facing threats from illegal logging, deforestation, and development projects like road construction and mini-hydropower plants. These activities are impacting the forest's biodiversity and ecosystems. Despite legal protections, enforcement issues persist, and conservationists are calling for stronger measures to safeguard the forest​. Groundviews )​( Groundviews

SIGIRIYA

Recent news about Sigiriya includes its development as Sri Lanka's first sustainable tourism destination, focusing on eco-friendly practices to protect the site and its surroundings​( Ceylon Independent ). Additionally, a new water plant funded by India has been installed to provide clean drinking water to visitors​( Newswire ). However, there was a safety incident in August 2024 when 22 tourists were hospitalized after a wasp attack at the site.

ELLA

The small hill town of Ella continues to grow in popularity, with its picturesque landscapes, hiking trails like Ella Rock and Little Adam’s Peak, and the famous Nine Arches Bridge being top draws. New eco-lodges and wellness retreats are being developed to cater to growing demand.

YAALA NATIONAL PARK

Yala and Wilpattu National Parks: Wildlife conservation efforts are making these destinations more attractive for safari-goers. There have been recent improvements in eco-tourism infrastructure, including sustainable lodges and wildlife viewing platforms​.
